Understanding and Shaping Early Brain Development

I am pediatric physician scientist who uses neuroimaging to study critical periods of development in the fetal and infant brain. Through this understanding, I hope to design positive interventions to improve outcomes for children and families.

About Me

I am a pediatric physician scientist passionate about understanding how early experiences shape fetal and infant brain development. My research focuses on the critical periods of development and how environmental factors influence neurological outcomes in young patients.

I currently train at Children's National as part of their Pediatric Research Track Residency and R38 research fellow. I conduct research at the Developing Brain Institute within the Center for Prenatal, Neonatal, and Maternal Health Research headed by Dr. Catherine Limperopoulos.

I earned my MD/PhD degree at Georgetown University School of Medicine. My thesis work examined how sensory systems support language in adults under the mentorship of Dr. Maximilian Riesenhuber in the Laboratory for Computational Cognitive Neuroscience.
