About

Fetal and infant brain development establish the foundation for lifelong brain function, making this period one of extraordinary potential and profound vulnerability. Subtle perturbations in prenatal physiology or early-life experience can alter developmental trajectories long before clinical symptoms emerge. My research seeks to identify early neural markers of atypical development, understand the mechanisms that drive divergence, and define windows when intervention can improve outcomes.

I study populations at increased risk of neurodevelopmental disability—including infants with congenital heart disease and those born preterm. My work examines how altered physiology and early exposures shape brain organization and influence later neurodevelopmental outcomes. My approach integrates advanced fetal and neonatal neuroimaging, quantitative modeling of brain network dynamics, and longitudinal clinical assessment.

By characterizing normative developmental trajectories and identifying early markers of divergence, I aim to pinpoint critical windows when intervention can alter outcomes—moving from delayed diagnosis to proactive, personalized care. This work seeks to shift the paradigm from reaction to prevention, identifying children at risk before symptoms emerge, when the brain is most responsive to support. Ultimately, this research lays groundwork for tools that clinicians and families can use to promote healthier neurodevelopmental trajectories from the very beginning.

Research Projects

Congenital Heart Disease & Brain Development

Investigating how altered cardiovascular physiology in congenital heart disease affects fetal and infant brain development and long-term neurodevelopmental outcomes.

Preterm Birth & Brain Organization

Examining how preterm birth influences the emergence of functional brain networks and cortical maturation in early infancy.

Precision fMRI in Infants

Developing advanced neuroimaging approaches to characterize individual differences in infant brain function with high precision and reliability.

Sensory Systems & Language

Computational neuroscience approaches to understanding how sensory processing supports language acquisition and processing.
